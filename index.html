About Me

Throughout my life, I can always recall having a passion for science, technology, and the arts. The pursuit of engineering, by no coincidence, has proven to be one of my most enlightening and defining experiences. However, as I mature and become aware of the state of the world around me, I am beginning to realize that my career path constitutes incredible responsibilities. No matter the industry I find myself in, I will always be faced with decisions that will make a difference. My mission is to ensure that the difference I make is a positive one.

Today, I am a graduate mechanical engineer at Northeastern University researching the application of continuum mechanics to early-stage degenerative muscular disease detection. During my undergrad, I co-founded Massachusetts Materials Technologies LLC, an MIT-based technology start-up that specializes in testing materials using principles of contact mechanics. My role in the group is diverse, but officially I am a research engineer, patent lead, and safety officer. I plan to graduate with a Masters this spring, and return to industry full-time.

I hope you have the time to explore and enjoy this portfolio which may serve as a window into my experiences as a developing engineer. If you have additional questions about my interests and experiences, I'd enjoy hearing from you. My resume and contact information are available below.

Research

Dept. of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering

Pen Grip Force Mechanics • January 2017 - Present

Analytically developing mechanics equations describing the strain in a tubular pen based on various grip styles and forces. The goal is to determine the minimum number and location of strain gauges necessary to fully characterize the grip of a user, and then validate these results experimentally. The potential applications of this research include progressive degenerative muscular disease detection.
