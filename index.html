About Me

Throughout my life, I can always recall having a passion for science, technology, and the arts. The pursuit of engineering, by no cooincidence, has proven to be one of my most enlightening and defining experiences. However, as I matured and became aware of the state of the world around me, I realized that my career path consituted incredible responsibilities. No matter the industry I find myself in, I will always be faced with decisions that will make a difference. My mission is to ensure that the difference I make is a positive one.

Today, I am a graduate mechanical engineer at Northeastern University researching the application of continuum mechanics to early-stage degenerative muscular disease detection. During my undergrad, I co-founded Massachusetts Materials Technologies LLC, an MIT-based technology start-up that specializes in testing materials using principles of contact mechanics. My role in the group is diverse, but officially I am a research engineer, patent champion, and safety officer. In May 2017, I plan to graduate with a Masters and return to working full-time.

I hope you have the time to explore and enjoy this portfolio which, to the best of my ability, may serve as a window into my experiences as a young engineer. If you have additional questions, or maybe just want to chat, my resume and contact information are available below.

Research

Dept. of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering

Pen Grip Force Mechanics • January 2017 - Present

Analytically developing mechanics equations describing the strain in a tubular pen based on various grip styles and forces. The goal is to determine the minimum number and location of strain gauges neccesary to fully characterize the grip of a user, and then validate these results experimentally. The potential applications of this research include progressive degenerative muscular disease detection.

Fitts' Movement Control Lab Augment • May 2012 - August 2013

Developed, tested, and implemented an interactive iPad app for a graduate-level Human Machine Systems lab activity involving Fitts's Law of Movement Control. The purpose of the app was to reduce experiment time, eliminate human error, and compile data for later use by the students.

Work

Massachusetts Materials Technologies LLC

Co-founder, Research Engineer - Cambridge, MA • March 2015 - Present

Supporting the development of an in-situ material tester of structural steel hardness, strength, and ductility. Leading proof of concept research on method of non-destructive fracture toughness evaluation. Developed and continuing to maintain the on-site safety program, including Chemical Hygiene Plan. Drafting and filing text and figures for provisional and non-provisional utility patents. Developing and submitting NSF-SBIR project proposals.

Materials and Engineering Group LLC

Engineering Consultant - Cambride, MA • October 2013 - Present

Consulting as expert witness support for failure-based litigation cases up to $1 billion in losses. Visually inspecting and documenting evidence, both on-site and in the lab. Developing and implementing test protocols for testing evidence and customer samples. Designing and interpretting analytical finite element models as demonstrative evidence. Drafting memos and reports for clients on technical findings and expert opinions.

Keurig, Inc.

Brewer Engineering Co-op - Burlington, MA • January 2013 - August 2013

Developed automated life-cycle testing apparatuses utilizing open-source electronics and programming. Processed and interpretted test data for internal engineering R&D qualifications. Communicated findings with brewer engineer design team and international manufacturers. Incorporated test results into experimental brewer builds, including both hardware and software adjustments.
